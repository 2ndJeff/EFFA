Vision & Values

The principles that guide everything we build

Our Vision

We envision a future where tuition assistance works for all stakeholders: employers, their workforce, and the learning providers who educate this workforce. In this future, milestone-based scholarships decrease student debt while graduation rates improve. More students know where they'll work upon graduation, and more jobs are filled quickly with the right talent, leading to better job satisfaction, productivity and profitability. If we all work together, universities, their student bodies, and the employers who hire those students√¢‚Ç¨‚Äùfuture members of the US workforce√¢‚Ç¨‚Äùwill have the confidence of knowing they will be gainfully employed, while employers thrive because they will be able to hire and retain the right talent to drive the growth of their businesses.

Our Values

Become The One To Trust

We hold a sacred responsibility in reducing student debt while connecting talent to jobs. Overcoming inertia within the current system is job #1. Trust is hard won and easy to lose - so we must strive with consistency to earn it and keep it.

All Boats Rise Together

We believe in the stakeholder model approach. When a company focuses solely on a single stakeholder, to the exclusion of others, it puts the "trustworthiness" of the organization at risk. At EFFA, we consistently strive to earn our reputation as "the one to trust" in financial services and human capital.

Culture of: "GSD and Then Some (ATS)!"

Always over-deliver on any promise you make... and then some!

Always Learning

We are committed to a long-term cycle of consistent optimization. The more data we leverage, the more we learn, the better we get, and the greater the value we can create across all stakeholders - students, the universities that educate them, and the enterprises that employ them.

Think Globally, Solve Locally

We are both student-centric and employer focused. This means that although our purpose is to serve students, we must realize that this entails prioritizing the needs of both students and employers. We approach all dimensions of our platform with global need, scale and size in mind, while getting to know - and ensuring that - each stakeholder's requirements are met to achieve buy-in, effective usage, and a mutual cycle of benefit and success.

Workforce Wellbeing First

When we have the bandwidth to take care of our families and ourselves first, that wellbeing translates into creativity, inspiration and momentum. We are hard chargers, who get it done -and then some. This GSD posture is fueled by "Workforce Wellbeing First."

WHY WE BUILT EFFA

We Saw the Problems Firsthand

After years of building tuition assistance platforms and working with employers and universities, we identified the systemic issues preventing programs from delivering real value.

The System Was Broken for Everyone

In our experience building and operating tuition assistance programs serving millions of employees, we saw the same problems repeat across every implementation:

  • Employers wanted participation and ROI but couldn't get either. Manual processes drained HR teams while employees couldn't afford upfront costs‚Äîeven with 100% reimbursement promised.
  • Universities wanted to grow employer partnerships but TPAs took 20-45% fees and controlled the relationships. Administrative burden prevented scaling, and reimbursement models limited student enrollment.
  • Students needed education to advance but couldn't access the benefits supposedly available to them. The out-of-pocket barrier excluded the very people who needed help most.

We knew there had to be a better way. So we built it.

Our Mission

We are on a mission to reduce student debt by helping employers invest in the talent they wish to hire and retain‚Äîby making tuition assistance work for everyone: employers, universities, and the students they serve.

Job Ready: The EFFA Podcast

2 years, 4 executive roundtables and 35+ episodes

Employers agree that university students need more preparation to become ready for the workplace. This is evidenced by the high rate of voluntary turnover among new grads during their first year of employment.

Job Ready's editorial charter is to explore the education-to-employment gap through root causes of this high rate of turnover. Towards this end, we hold kitchen-table style conversations with the experts who are working to create solutions.
